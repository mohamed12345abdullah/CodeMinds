ROV 2017 Competition

The 2017 ROV ("Remotely Operated Vehicle") Competition, organized by the Marine Advanced Technology Education (MATE) Center, was a prestigious international event focused on promoting innovation in underwater robotics. Teams from around the world participated, showcasing their skills in designing, building, and operating ROVs to complete a series of complex underwater tasks. The competition aimed to inspire students to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) by providing a real-world context for learning and collaboration.

Minesweepers Competition

The Minesweepers Competition is an international event aimed at developing advanced robotic solutions for the detection and clearance of landmines and unexploded ordnances. This competition encourages innovation and collaboration among teams from around the world, focusing on creating safe, effective, and efficient robotic systems that can operate in challenging environments. By bringing together participants with diverse skills in robotics, electronics, and software engineering, the Minesweepers Competition plays a crucial role in advancing technology for humanitarian demining efforts.

Micromouse Competition

The Micromouse competition involves small autonomous robots navigating a 16×16 maze grid. Each cell in the maze is 180 mm square with 50 mm high walls. The robots start from a predetermined position and aim to reach the central area of the maze independently. They track their position, discover walls as they explore, and recognize when they reach the goal. After finding the goal, robots usually conduct additional searches to identify the optimal route. Once the optimal path is determined, the robots race to complete the maze in the shortest possible time.
